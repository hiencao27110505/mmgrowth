How to write a great idea

Inspired by Amazon's working-backwards memos. Aim for clarity, not length — your reader is busy.

What — the proposed initiative

One sentence: "We will [verb] [noun] so that [outcome]."

Skip vague verbs (improve, enhance, optimize) and marketing fluff (delightful, world-class, seamless). Replace them with the user-visible change.

Why — the customer problem

Start with the customer, not the business goal.

Quantify: drop-off %, NPS delta, support tickets, revenue at risk.

Avoid weasel words (might, perhaps, we believe, có lẽ) — claim it confidently. Pass the "so what?" test.

How — the proposed approach

High-level direction, not implementation tickets.

Name the MVP scope and what's deliberately out.

Skip corporate jargon (synergy, leverage, ecosystem, holistic). Mention 1–2 alternatives you considered.

Brief like you're explaining to your boss in 60 seconds.
